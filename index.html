OpenBookMap

A global atlas · of used bookstores · and the books inside them
Open the Map →

We are building an open, community-made map of every used bookstore in the world — and, shelf by shelf, a searchable index of the books on their shelves. Built on the OpenStreetMap ecosystem, in the spirit of OpenSkiMap. Free to use. Free to contribute. Forever.

The IdeaI.

Find the book before you drive two hours to the shop.

Used bookstores are one of the last great analog pleasures — and one of the worst for discovery. You can't search their shelves from your phone. You drive across town on a hunch. Sometimes you find a treasure. Most times you don't.

OpenBookMap is the shared index we've all been missing. Contributors drop a pin on a shop, snap a few photos of the shelves, and our system turns those photos into a searchable catalog. Next traveler searches a title — and knows exactly which shop, in which city, on which shelf, to walk into.

How It WorksII.
Step 01

Map the shop

Contributors add used bookstores to OpenStreetMap — name, hours, a short description. Every shop gets a pin on our map.

Step 02

Photograph the shelves

Walk in, snap spines, upload. No special gear. A phone is enough. Photos stay linked to their shop and shelf.

Step 03

Extract the titles

Our pipeline turns shelf photos into a list of titles and authors — searchable in the browser, in seconds, worldwide.

Step 04

Search the world

Looking for a first-edition Calvino? Find the three shops that have one — and plan your next detour.
